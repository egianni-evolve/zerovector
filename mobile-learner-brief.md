# Who Is the Mobile Learner?

**Brief based on mobile QA observations, March 2026**

---

## The Profile

The mobile learner on Open Vector is not someone sitting down for a study session. They are someone who found a link, tapped it, and is now deciding in the next 10 seconds whether this is worth their time.

They are likely:

- **A curious newcomer** who encountered Zero Vector through a social post, a shared link, or a search result. They didn't plan to learn today. They're browsing.
- **On their phone because that's what they have.** Not because they chose mobile over desktop. For many learners, the phone is the primary (or only) device. Assuming "they'll switch to desktop later" means assuming access they may not have.
- **Mid-context.** They're on a bus, in a waiting room, between tasks. Attention is fractured. They will not scroll through blank space or debug a broken nav. They will leave.
- **Not a developer yet.** Open Vector's curriculum starts at "The Terminal" — orientation-level content. These learners don't know what a code block should look like, which makes formatting issues (Issue 10) more confusing, not less.

---

## What They're Trying to Do

1. **Figure out what this is.** The /open landing page is their first impression. Does this look real? Is it for them? Can they trust it?
2. **Read one lesson.** They want a taste. If the first lesson is readable and makes them feel smart, they'll come back. If it loads mid-sentence, they won't.
3. **Decide if they'll return.** This is the real conversion event. Not signup, not completion — the decision to bookmark or remember the URL.

---

## What Mobile Gets Wrong Today

The content quality is high. The mobile experience doesn't match. Specifically:

- **The first impression is broken.** Pages load at wrong scroll positions, so the learner's first experience is confusion.
- **Navigation is unreliable.** The sidebar bleeds through, making it hard to explore. A learner who can't browse can't get hooked.
- **The content assumes desktop.** Code blocks expect wide viewports. Exercises are formatted as prose paragraphs. These work on a 1440px monitor. They don't work on a 360px phone.
- **There's no mobile-aware flow.** No progressive disclosure, no "start here" prompt, no acknowledgment that the learner might be on a small screen.

---

## The Opportunity

Mobile learners are the top of the funnel. They are the people who haven't committed yet. Every mobile friction point is a lost potential learner who never comes back.

Fixing the three critical issues (scroll position, sidebar nav, blank gaps) would make Open Vector functional on mobile. Adding a mobile-first onboarding flow would make it welcoming. The content is already strong enough to do the rest.

---

*K-007 observation: The site currently serves desktop learners who already decided to learn. The mobile experience needs to serve people who haven't decided yet.*
